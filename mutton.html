Mutton Biryani

Mutton Biryani is a regal celebration of slow-cooked tender meat and fragrant basmati rice. Marinated in aromatic spices and layered with herbs, the mutton absorbs deep, bold flavors during dum cooking.

Key Ingredients:

Basmati rice, Mutton, Yogurt, Onion, Ginger-garlic paste, Green chilies, Mint leaves, Salt, Cooking Oil, Coriander leaves, Whole spices, Biryani masala, Red chili powder, Turmeric powder, Ghee, Oil, Lemon juice, Salt.

Quantity: 1 bowl

No. of Orders

Description

1. Mutton is cleaned thoroughly and cut into medium pieces.
2. Mutton is marinated with yogurt, spices, ginger-garlic paste, and lemon juice.
3. The marination is rested to allow flavors to penetrate deeply.
4. Basmati rice is washed and soaked for fragrance.
5. Rice is parboiled with whole spices until partially cooked.
6. Onions are deep-fried until golden and crisp.
7. Ghee is heated in a heavy-bottomed pot.
8. Marinated mutton is layered at the bottom of the pot.
9. Parboiled rice is layered gently over the mutton.
10. Fried onions, mint, and coriander are sprinkled generously.
11. Ghee is drizzled over the layers for richness.
12. The pot is sealed tightly for dum cooking.
13. Biryani is slow-cooked on low heat until the mutton turns tender.
14. Gently mixed and served hot with raita or salan.
